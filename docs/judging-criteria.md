# Judging Criteria

Projects are scored by a judging panel on five criteria, **equally weighted (20 points each, 100 total)**.

## 1. Problem & relevance (20)

Did you pick a real problem within your track, and frame it sharply?

- 17–20: A specific, well-motivated problem; obvious who the user is and why it matters
- 10–16: A real problem, but broad or loosely framed
- 0–9: Vague problem, or a solution looking for a problem

## 2. Technical execution (20)

Does the prototype actually work?

- 17–20: Runs end to end; thoughtful architecture; handles the demo path robustly
- 10–16: Core flow works with rough edges
- 0–9: Mostly mockups, or breaks during the demo

## 3. Use of AI (20)

Is AI doing real work, or decorating the UI?

- 17–20: AI is central — reasoning, matching, generating, or deciding — and its output is meaningfully better than a rules-based baseline
- 10–16: AI adds value but is replaceable
- 0–9: AI is cosmetic or absent

## 4. Demo quality (20)

Does your submitted demo make it click?

- 17–20: Clear story; a deployed tool that's obvious to use, or a tight video; the "aha" lands without you explaining it live
- 10–16: Understandable demo with some friction
- 0–9: Confusing, over time, or no working demo

## 5. Potential impact (20)

Could this matter beyond today?

- 17–20: Credible path to real users; clearly extensible beyond the sample data
- 10–16: Plausible value with significant open questions
- 0–9: Hard to see who would use it

## How judging works

1. Submissions close at the announced deadline — only Issues submitted in time are judged.
2. Each team is judged from its submission — deployed link, demo video, and repo. There is no live demo or Q&A.
3. Judges score independently; scores are averaged.
4. Track winners are the top-scoring projects within each track; **Best Overall** is the top score across all tracks.
5. **Best Use of Cursor** is awarded by the Cursor team for the most impressive development workflow.

## Tie-breakers

In order: Use of AI score, Technical execution score, judges' vote.

## Notes for teams

- Judges score what they see, not what you describe as future work.
- Hardcoding a happy path for the demo is fine; claiming it's more than it is, is not.
- Honesty about limitations reads as strength, not weakness.
